# Open AIM – Founding Record

## Overview

This document records the origins of the Open AIM Project and preserves the history of its creation.

Open AIM was established as an open, community-driven initiative with the goal of developing a vendor-neutral specification that enables websites to communicate structured, trustworthy information to AI systems and other software.

As the project grows, this document serves as a historical record of how the project began.

## Founding Vision

To create an open, secure and vendor-neutral specification that enables websites to publish structured, machine-readable metadata that improves understanding between websites, AI systems and software.

Open AIM is founded on the belief that openness, transparency and interoperability are essential for the future of the web.

---

## Mission

To develop an open specification that allows websites to communicate structured, verifiable information in a consistent, extensible and trustworthy manner.

## Project Origins

The initial concept for Open AIM originated from discussions exploring how websites could communicate more effectively with modern AI systems.

During these discussions it became clear that, while websites already use technologies such as robots.txt, XML sitemaps and structured data, there was an opportunity to explore a complementary open specification focused on providing clear, structured and trustworthy metadata for AI systems and other software.

Rather than creating a proprietary solution, the decision was made to establish Open AIM as an open, community-driven project that could evolve through public discussion and collaboration.

## Governance Philosophy

Although Open AIM records its origins, it is intended to become a community-driven project.

The long-term success of Open AIM depends on contributions from developers, researchers, standards bodies, content creators, website owners and the wider open-source community.

No individual or organization should be considered the sole voice of the specification. Open AIM is intended to evolve through open discussion, technical merit and community consensus.
